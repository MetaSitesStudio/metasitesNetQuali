What is SpeedFox?

SpeedFox is a free, browser-based internet performance analyzer that goes far beyond traditional speed tests. Built by MetaSites Studio, it measures not just download and upload speeds, but also connection stability, latency under load, packet integrity, DNS performance, and real-world streaming quality — all in a single 30-second test with no signup required.

How SpeedFox Works

SpeedFox runs a multi-phase diagnostic test directly in your browser:

  1. Speed Analysis — Measures download and upload throughput using multi-stream adaptive testing for accurate results even on fast connections.
  2. Latency Profiling — Tests ping response time, jitter variance, and latency stability under both idle and loaded conditions.
  3. Stability Assessment — Detects packet loss, measures bufferbloat (latency increase under load), and evaluates connection consistency.
  4. Streaming Quality Estimation — Determines the maximum video streaming resolution your connection can reliably sustain (480p, 720p, 1080p, 4K).
  5. DNS Performance — Measures DNS resolution speed, which directly impacts how fast websites begin loading.

The entire test takes approximately 30 seconds and produces a comprehensive quality score along with detailed metric breakdowns.

What SpeedFox Measures

Download Speed

How fast data travels from the internet to your device, measured in Mbps. Essential for streaming, downloading files, and browsing.

Upload Speed

How fast data travels from your device to the internet, measured in Mbps. Critical for video calls, cloud backups, and sharing content.

Ping / Latency

The round-trip time for data packets, measured in milliseconds. Low latency is crucial for gaming, video conferencing, and real-time applications.

Jitter

The variation in latency over time. High jitter causes choppy audio/video and inconsistent gaming performance, even when average ping is low.

Packet Loss

The percentage of data packets that fail to reach their destination. Even 1% packet loss can significantly degrade VoIP calls and online gaming.

Bufferbloat

The increase in latency when your connection is under heavy load. High bufferbloat means your internet feels slow during peak usage — video calls freeze while someone else streams Netflix.

DNS Resolution Speed

How quickly your DNS server translates domain names into IP addresses. Slow DNS adds delay to every website you visit and every app you open.

Video Streaming Quality

SpeedFox estimates the maximum video resolution your connection can reliably sustain, from 480p SD to 4K Ultra HD, based on actual throughput and stability.
